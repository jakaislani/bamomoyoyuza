An American football running back for the Cleveland Browns of the National Football League (NFL), Jerome Ford salary for the 2023 season is $870,000.

Just two seasons in the league, Ford is already making money hand over fist.

He has already made $1.82 from salaries and incentives.

Ford began his football journey at Armwood High School in Seffner, Florida, where he showcased his talent and dedication to the sport.

His exceptional skills and potential earned him a spot at the University of Alabama, where he committed to playing college football.

From 2018 to 2019, Ford donned the crimson jersey of the University of Alabama, leaving his mark on the field.

During these two years, he demonstrated his prowess by rushing for a total of 151 yards on 31 carries, resulting in an impressive three touchdowns.

In 2020, Ford embarked on a new chapter in his college football career, transferring to the University of Cincinnati.

This transition allowed him to shine as he embraced his role as a key contributor to the team.

In his debut year with Cincinnati, in 2020, Ford played in 10 games, primarily serving as the backup to the talented Gerrid Doaks.

Ford’s remarkable performance was highlighted by his impressive rushing stats, amassing 483 yards on 73 carries and achieving eight impressive touchdowns.

Jerome Ford Salary

The Cleveland Browns running back Jerome Ford salary for the 2023 season is $870,000.

He was drafted by the Browns on April 30, 2022, and signed a four-year $3.98 million contract in May.

This four-year contract includes a $322,148 signing bonus, $322,148 guaranteed, and an average annual salary of $995,537.

In 2023, Ford will earn a base salary of $870,000 while carrying a cap hit of $950,537 and a dead cap value of $241,611.

Thus far, Ford has bagged $1.82 million in salaries and signing bonuses, and with the conclusion of his ongoing deal, he will have made $3.90 million from the NFL alone.

Jerome Ford To Be No. 1 RB after Nick Chubb Injury

Following the unfortunate season-ending injury to All-Pro running back Nick Chubb, Browns coach Kevin Stefanski made it clear on Tuesday that Jerome Ford has now assumed the role of the team’s featured running back.

Chubb sustained a left knee injury during the second quarter of Monday night’s game against the Steelers, requiring him to be carted off the field.

While Coach Stefanski refrained from providing specific details about Chubb’s injury, he did confirm that Chubb would need to undergo surgery.

Coach Stefanski also mentioned that the Browns are actively exploring options to bolster their running back depth by adding another player to the roster.

Nevertheless, he emphasized that Ford would step into the primary running back role previously occupied by Chubb.

As part of their efforts to address the situation, free agent running back Kareem Hunt, who had spent the last four seasons with the Browns, visited the team on Tuesday, as reported by sources to ESPN’s Jeremy Fowler.
