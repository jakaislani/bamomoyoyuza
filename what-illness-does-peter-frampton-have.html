Musician Peter Frampton Health Problems And Sickness

On February 22, 2019, Peter Frampton announced that he would be retiring from touring with his farewell tour, ‘Peter Frampton-The Farewell Tour’ commencing on June 18, 2019, in Tulsa, Oklahoma, running through October 12, concluding in Concord, California.

The musician also revealed the reason behind the farewell tour, and he was diagnosed with inclusion body myositis (IBM), a progressive muscle disorder. The tour featured special guests Jason Bonham’s Led Zeppelin Evening and Peter’s son.

Each dollar of every ticket sold for the tour was donated to benefit Peter’s newly formed myositis research fund at Johns Hopkins, where he is being treated. In June 2019, his album, All Blues, featured at number one on the Billboard Top Blues Albums Chart.

In December 2019, Frampton further announced his farewell U.K. tour to consist of five performances in May 2020. However, the UK/EU was canceled in April due to COVID-19.

Where Is Peter Frampton Now?

Peter Frampton is doing his best, establishing the Peter Frampton Myositis Research Fund at John Hopkins to raise research on the disease. IBM is pretty rare, merely affecting maybe 8 out of every 1 million Americans.

IBM has not collected enough research funding, nor has it been a big focus of drug development by pharmaceutical companies. The musician is doing his best to change this situation.

In February 2019, Peter appeared on the hit T.V. show CBS This Morning to announce the fund and his 51-date U.S. farewell tour. He added that each promoter donated a dollar from every ticket bought.
